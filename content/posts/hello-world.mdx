How to Connect Zerodha Kite to Wicklog
Step 1 — Open Integrations in your Wicklog settings
Log into your Wicklog account. Go to Settings in the top navigation and click the Integrations tab. You will see Zerodha Kite listed as an available integration.
> 📸 *[Screenshot: Wicklog Settings → Integrations page showing the Zerodha Kite connect button]*
Step 2 — Authorize the connection
Click Connect Zerodha Kite. You will be redirected to Zerodha's official login page. Enter your Kite credentials and complete the 2FA (TOTP or SMS). Zerodha will then ask you to approve Wicklog's read-only access to your trade data. Click Authorize.
> 📸 *[Screenshot: Zerodha Kite authorization screen showing read-only access permissions]*
Step 3 — Fetch your trades
You'll be redirected back to Wicklog automatically. Now click Fetch Trades, choose your date range, and hit Import. Your trades will appear in your journal within seconds.
> 📸 *[Screenshot: Wicklog journal showing imported trades from Kite]*
That's it. You're set up. From here, click any trade to open the journal editor and start writing.

How Secure Is the Kite Integration?
Security is something we take seriously at Wicklog, and we want to be fully transparent about how this works.
Read-only access — always
When you authorize Wicklog via the Kite Connect API, you are granting read-only permission. This means Wicklog can only read your trade history. It cannot:
Place orders
Modify or cancel orders
Access your funds or holdings for transactions
Transfer money in any form
This is enforced at the API level by Zerodha itself — not just a promise we make. Zerodha's OAuth-based authorization system strictly limits what each connected app can do, and Wicklog only requests the minimum permissions required.
Your Zerodha password never touches Wicklog
The authorization happens entirely on Zerodha's own servers. You log in directly on Zerodha's official page — Wicklog never sees your Kite username, password, or TOTP. What Wicklog receives is a short-lived access token issued by Zerodha after you authorize. This is the same standard used by banks and major financial apps (OAuth 2.0).
You can revoke access any time
You are always in control. To disconnect Wicklog from your Kite account:
Go to your Zerodha Kite account → Apps & Permissions
Find Wicklog and click Revoke Access
Or disconnect from within Wicklog via Settings → Integrations → Disconnect.
Once revoked, Wicklog loses all ability to access your Kite data immediately.

How We Store Your Trade Data
We believe you deserve to know exactly what happens to your data after it's imported.
What we store: When you fetch trades, Wicklog stores the trade details — symbol, quantity, price, timestamps, and P&L — along with any journal notes, tags, and screenshots you add. This is the data that powers your stats dashboard and makes your journal searchable.
What we don't store: We do not store your Kite login credentials, your Kite password, or any sensitive account information. The access token issued by Zerodha expires automatically and is not retained beyond the active session.
Where your data lives: All Wicklog data is stored on secure cloud servers with encryption at rest and in transit (HTTPS/TLS). We do not sell your data to third parties. Your journal is private — only you can see it.
Data portability: You own your data. You can export your full journal and trade history from Wicklog at any time from Settings → Export Data. We will never hold your data hostage.

Frequently Asked Questions
Does Wicklog work with Zerodha Coin or Zerodha's mutual fund investments?
Currently, the integration fetches equity and F&O trades from your Kite trading account. Mutual fund data from Coin is not included.
Can I fetch historical trades from before I signed up for Wicklog?
Yes. You can import trades from any date range available in your Kite account history, even if those trades were made before you created your Wicklog account.
Will new trades sync automatically after I fetch once?
Trades are not synced in real-time. You manually click Fetch Trades whenever you want to import new data. This keeps you in control of when your journal updates, and prevents cluttered entries from partial-day trades.
What happens to my journal notes if I re-fetch trades?
Nothing. Existing journal entries, tags, screenshots, and notes are never overwritten when you re-fetch. New trades are simply added to your journal.
Is the Kite integration available on mobile?
Yes. Wicklog's web app is fully responsive and works on mobile browsers. Fetch trades and write journal entries from your phone right after the market closes.
Does this work with Zerodha Kite for NRI accounts?
We currently support resident Indian Zerodha accounts. NRI account support is on our roadmap — follow our updates at wicklog.in.
What if I use multiple brokers?
If you also trade on other platforms, you can still manually add those trades to Wicklog. We're working on integrations with more brokers — Kite is the first of many.
