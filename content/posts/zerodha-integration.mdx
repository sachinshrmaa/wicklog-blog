## How to Connect Zerodha Kite to Wicklog

### Step 1 — Open Integrations in Wicklog settings

Log into your Wicklog account. Go to **Settings** in the top navigation and click the **Integrations** tab. You will see Zerodha Kite listed as an available integration.

![Wicklog Settings → Integrations tab showing the Zerodha Kite connect button](/blog/images/kite-integration-settings.png)

### Step 2 — Authorize the connection

Click **Connect Zerodha Kite**. You'll be redirected to Zerodha's official login page. Enter your Kite credentials and complete 2FA (TOTP or SMS). Zerodha will ask you to approve Wicklog's read-only access. Click **Authorize**.

![Zerodha Kite OAuth authorization screen showing read-only permissions for Wicklog](/blog/images/kite-oauth-authorize.png)

### Step 3 — Fetch your trades

You'll be redirected back to Wicklog automatically. Click **Fetch Trades**, choose your date range, and hit **Import**. Your trades will appear in your journal within seconds.

![Wicklog journal showing successfully imported trades from Zerodha Kite](/blog/images/kite-trades-imported.png)

That's it. From here, click any trade to open the journal editor and start writing.

## How Secure Is the Kite Integration?

Security is something we take seriously at Wicklog. Here's exactly how it works.

### Read-only access — always

When you authorize Wicklog, you grant **read-only** permission. Wicklog can **only read** your trade history. It **cannot**:

- Place, modify, or cancel orders
- Access your funds or holdings for transactions
- Transfer money in any form

This is enforced at the API level by Zerodha — not just a promise we make.

### Your Kite password never touches Wicklog

Authorization happens entirely on Zerodha's own servers. You log in directly on Zerodha's page — Wicklog never sees your username, password, or TOTP. Wicklog only receives a **short-lived access token** issued by Zerodha after you authorize. This is the same OAuth 2.0 standard used by banks and major financial apps.

### Revoke access any time

You are always in control. To disconnect:

1. Go to Zerodha Kite → **Apps & Permissions**
2. Find Wicklog and click **Revoke Access**

Or disconnect directly from **Wicklog → Settings → Integrations → Disconnect**.

Once revoked, Wicklog loses all access to your Kite data immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Wicklog work with Zerodha Coin (mutual funds)?**
Not yet. The integration currently fetches equity and F&O trades from your Kite trading account. Coin support is on the roadmap.

**Can I import trades from before I signed up for Wicklog?**
Yes. You can import from any date range available in your Kite account history — even trades made years ago.

**Will trades sync automatically going forward?**
Trades are not synced in real-time. You click Fetch Trades whenever you want to import. This keeps you in control and prevents cluttered entries from partial-day trades.

**What if I re-fetch a date range I already imported?**
Existing journal entries, notes, tags, and screenshots are never overwritten. New trades are simply added.

**Does this work on mobile?**
Yes. Wicklog is fully responsive. Fetch trades and journal from your phone right after the market closes.

**What about NRI accounts?**
We currently support resident Indian Zerodha accounts. NRI support is on the roadmap.

**I also trade on other brokers. Can I still use Wicklog?**
Yes. You can add trades from other brokers manually. More broker integrations are coming — Kite is the first.
